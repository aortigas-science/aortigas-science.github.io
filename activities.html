Outreach

Service & Community Engagement

Beyond my research and teaching, I am committed to advancing inclusivity and participation in science. I have been involved in several organizations and initiatives aimed at fostering a more diverse and welcoming scientific community, particularly for underrepresented students pursuing graduate education and research careers.

Summer Research Opportunities Program (SROP) Mentor

Graduate Student Mentor ‚Ä¢ 2025-Present

This year, I served as a graduate student mentor for the 2025 Summer Research Opportunities Program, which aims to increase the number of underrepresented students who are pursuing graduate study and research careers. As part of this experience, I mentored Braylen Washington, an undergraduate student from the University of Florida, on a research project involving varicella-zoster virus genome sequencing. Braylen and I will now be co-authors on an upcoming publication, and he was recently selected as a McNair Scholar.

Key Activities: Research mentorship, career guidance, graduate school preparation

Braylen presenting our VZV research at the 2025 Annual Biomedical Research Conference for Minoritized Scientists (ABRCMS)

SACNAS National Conference Representative

Bioinformatics & Genomics Program Representative ‚Ä¢ 2023

Selected as the Bioinformatics and Genomics program representative for the Penn State delegation to the Society for the Advancement of Chicanos/Hispanics and Native Americans in Science (SACNAS) National Conference. Responsibilities included setting up the promotional booth, helping to promote Penn State graduate programs to undergraduate students from diverse and underrepresented backgrounds, and sharing my own experiences as an international student with potential applicants.

This experience allowed me to connect with prospective students and help them navigate the graduate school application process while promoting diversity in STEM fields.
