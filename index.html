PhD Candidate in the Szpara Lab at The Pennsylvania State University

Szpara Lab
Szpara Lab
The Pennsylvania State University

I use computational and genomic approaches to understand herpesvirus diversity and evolution. My work combines high-fidelity long-read sequencing with bioinformatics to address fundamental questions about viral genetic variation, particularly in tandem repeat regions. I am particularly interested in how genomic variation contributes to viral virulence and vaccine escape, with a focus on avian herpesviruses like Marek's disease virus. Prior to joining Penn State, I completed my HBSc in Bioinformatics at Lakehead University in 2021, where I worked with Dr. Ingeborg Zehbe.
